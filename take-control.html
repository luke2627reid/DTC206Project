Your Action Plan

Take Control

You can't stop companies from having privacy policies, but you can limit what they collect and see exactly what they already have. Here are the official tools from each company — use them.

Google

Google Takeout ↗ Privacy Checkup ↗
  1. Go to takeout.google.com and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Select which Google services you want to see data from — Gmail, Maps, Search, YouTube, and more.
  3. Download your archive. You may be surprised how much is there.
  4. Visit the Privacy Checkup link to adjust what Google saves about you going forward, including Location History, Web & App Activity, and ad personalization.

Apple

Apple Data & Privacy ↗
  1. Go to privacy.apple.com and sign in with your Apple ID.
  2. Select "Get a copy of your data."
  3. Choose which data categories to include — App Store activity, Apple Pay, iCloud, and more — and submit your request.
  4. Apple will email you within 7 days when your download is ready.

Meta (Facebook / Instagram)

Meta Privacy Center ↗
  1. Go to facebook.com/privacy/center and sign in.
  2. Use the Privacy Checkup tool to review who sees your posts and personal info.
  3. Go to Settings → Your Facebook Information → Download Your Information to get a copy of your data.
  4. Review which apps have access to your account and revoke any you don't recognize.

Microsoft

Privacy Dashboard ↗
  1. Go to account.microsoft.com/privacy and sign in with your Microsoft account.
  2. Review your browsing history, search history, and location activity.
  3. Use the "Clear" options to delete categories of data you don't want Microsoft to retain.
  4. Adjust ad settings to limit personalized advertising across Microsoft properties.

TikTok

TikTok Privacy Settings ↗
  1. Open TikTok and go to Profile → Menu → Settings and Privacy.
  2. Tap Privacy and review who can see your activity, likes, and following list.
  3. Go to Ads and Data → Off-TikTok Activity to limit tracking on other websites.
  4. Request a copy of your data from Settings → Privacy → Personalization and Data.
Quick Wins

Do These Right Now

Five changes you can make in the next 15 minutes that meaningfully reduce how much data is being collected about you.

  • Turn off ad personalization in your Google account settings at myaccount.google.com → Data & Privacy → Ad Settings.
  • On iPhone, go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking and turn off "Allow Apps to Request to Track."
  • On Facebook, go to Settings → Off-Facebook Activity and clear your history to disconnect past activity from your profile.
  • On TikTok, go to Settings → Privacy → Ads and Data and opt out of personalized ads and off-TikTok activity tracking.
  • On any browser, install a free tracker blocker like uBlock Origin — it stops most third-party trackers before they can collect your data.
